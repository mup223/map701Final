WWII Persuasive Cartography

Since there is a date range of 1940-1949 on this map, it is difficult to determine what the overarching intention was, other than the content presented. By this, I mean, if this map was produced in 1940, one could argue that it was a ploy to induce support for the US enrance into the foreign conflict (prior to Pearl Harbor obviously). If it was produced after 1941, then one could surmise that it's intent is merly to give perspective and scale to just how successful and serious the German occupation was.

As noted in Tyner's Persuasive Cartography, WWII increased interest in maps, and the power of propoganda and pursuasive narratives in cartography. Rererncing Hans Speier in Tyner's article, he says that propoganda maps are used as "blueprints of the future". It is my opinion that a map like this is surely intended to induce fear that the Germans could come to America and do the same thing they'd been successful at doing on the Asian continent. A sort of "blueprint" for the minds of ordinary people to help them understand the scale and reality of Germany's potential.
